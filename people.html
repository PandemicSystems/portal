Project Council

Justin Bahl

Justin Bahl is an Associate Professor at the University of Georgia with appointments in the College of Public Health, College of Veterinary Medicine, and Institute of Bioinformatics.
Having completed a B.S. with Honors at the University of Toronto, he pursued a PhD in Molecular Systematics and Evolution at the University of Hong Kong. With Hong Kong as the epicenter of the 2003 SARS-CoV global epidemic, Justin changed his research focus to ecology and evolution of infectious diseases.

Read more

After completing his PhD, he joined the State Key Laboratory for Emerging Infectious Diseases as a postdoctoral fellow and researched viral ecology and evolution. The State Key Laboratory is a World Health Organization (WHO) reference laboratory for the diagnosis of influenza A/H5 and is part of the NIAID Centers for Excellence for Influenza Research and Surveillance. He later moved to Singapore to continue his postdoctoral training in Molecular Epidemiology at Duke-NUS Graduate Medical School and was promoted to Assistant Professor. He later joined the University of Texas School of Public Health in Houston as an Associate Professor before relocating to the University of Georgia. His training in evolutionary biology, epidemiology and ecology has equipped him for a research career focused on infectious disease dynamics.

John Drake

John Drake is Regents Professor of Ecology, Odum School of Ecology and Director of the Center for the Ecology of Infectious Diseases (CEID) and the Global Infectious Disease Intelligence Consortium (GIDIC), University of Georgia.A biological modeler who studies the dynamics of zoonotic infectious diseases, John has previously used spatial interaction models to understand and predict the spread of White-nose syndrome in the North America, the 2013-2015 West African Ebola epidemic, SARS-CoV-2 across the United States in 2020, and COVID-19 importation risk into United States port of entry airports.

Read more

His research additionally seeds to understand how zoonotic Infectious Disease Intelligence can be used to inform leaders when an outbreak is beggingging, and how it may spread.As the PIPP Grant Phase I Principal Investigator (PI), John is leading a team of five scientists who are associated with the Cary Institute of Ecosystem Studies, Department of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Michigan, and several colleges and schools at the University of Georgia.John holds a PhD in Biological Sciences from Notre Dame University.

Bogdan Epureanu

Bogdan I. Epureanu is an Arthur F. Thurnau Professor in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Michigan and has a courtesy appointment in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. He is the Director of the Automotive Research Center (ARC), a $50 million center for innovating cutting-edge technologies that serve the next generation of ground transportation systems. The ARC is the flagship academic partner of the U.S. Army Ground Vehicle Systems Center, and at the heart of an ecosystem of research and education.

Read more

Bogdan has earned an international reputation for modeling the dynamics of an exceptionally broad range of complex, nonlinear systems. His work is notable in its real-world impact on aerospace, ground transportation and energy, as well as biologic and ecologic systems. Many of Bogdan’s algorithms and models of the dynamics of airplane engine components have been implemented into manufacturing processes by long standing industry partners, enabling next-generation turbo-jet engines for both military and commercial aircraft. The methods he developed to use vibration to assess the health of lithium-ion battery packs have led to new design approaches to prolong battery life in hybrid electric vehicles. His work elucidating how structures interact with complex materials has led to new approaches to building foundations for offshore wind turbines. He has published over 350 articles in journals, conferences, and books.He received his Ph.D. from Duke University in 1999.

Barbara Han

Dr. Barbara Han is a disease ecologist at the Cary Institute of Ecosystem Studies (NY). Her research program at the Cary Institute uses machine learning, deep learning, ecoinformatics, and dynamical modeling approaches with the goal to generate actionable predictions about infectious disease spillover and disease transmission in human and animal species.

Read more

Barbara completed a B.S. in Natural Science at Pepperdine University, and a PhD in Zoology at Oregon State University. During her PhD, she also spent a year as a Fulbright Fellow in Caracas, Venezuela. Dr. Han went on to complete consecutive postdoctoral fellowships in biological informatics (sponsored by NSF) and machine learning (sponsored by NIH) at the University of Georgia, Odum School of Ecology.

Pejman Rohani

Pejman Rohani is Regent’s Professor of Ecology and Associate Dean for Academic Affairs, Odum School of Ecology, and Deputy Director of the Center for Influenza Disease and Emergence Research at the University of Georgia.

Read more

Pejman’s research is focused on population biology, usually of host-natural interactions, with a view to understanding fundamental processes in ecology and evolution. He uses a combination of mathematical modeling, data analysis and statistical inference to understand the ecology and evolution of infectious diseases of humans and wildlife, including childhood infections and emerging infectious diseases. Pejman holds a PhD from Imperial College, University of London.
